HeritageHarvest Capital — Business Plan

1. Executive Summary
HeritageHarvest Capital is a Zambia-based agrifund and micro-lending business that combines smallholder poultry production with a short-term lending pool to local entrepreneurs. The model targets retired investors seeking conservative, tangible returns. Founder: Allen Baker.

2. Opportunity
High local demand for poultry products and strong need for short-term credit to small traders. Combining both diversifies risk and produces steady cash flow.

3. Model
• 60% of investor funds => Poultry: feed, chicks, housing improvements, veterinary.
• 40% of investor funds => Micro-loans: short-term (30–90 days) to vetted small businesses at interest rates that create net yield for investors.
• Fees: Management fee 8% of net profit for operations and reporting.

4. Target Market
Retired individuals and community groups seeking low-risk, steady returns and tangible assets.

5. Operations
• Farm operations: scale existing 10x5 chicken run into 3 cohorts of 200 birds each (by expansion).
• Lending: local loan officers, KYC, basic credit scoring, collateral where possible.
• Reporting: monthly production and loan performance dashboards.

6. Financial Projections (example for first 12 months)
Assume $10,000 raised in Round 1.
• Poultry (60% = $6,000): Projection net yield after costs = 20% annually => $1,200
• Micro-lending (40% = $4,000): Net yield after defaults = 12% annually => $480
Gross return = $1,680 -> 16.8% on $10k. After management and reserve buffers (target 8% to investors), projected conservative payout = 6–8% per cycle.

7. Risk & Mitigation
• Biosecurity and vet support for poultry.
• Loan diversification and strict vetting.
• Reserve fund to cover unexpected losses.

8. Use of funds
• Infrastructure (feed storage, coop repairs) 30%
• Chicks & feed 25%
• Loan capital 40%
• Admin & reporting 5%

9. Exit & Liquidity
• Quarterly redemptions with 30 days notice (subject to liquidity and operational cashflow).
• Priority buyback from operating profits.

10. Governance
• Quarterly investor reports.
• Advisory committee with external agronomist and finance advisor.
